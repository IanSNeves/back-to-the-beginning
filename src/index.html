Sell Your Soul to Metal

Legendary Tributes & The Final Farewell

Black Sabbath

The Final Curtain. For one last time, the original architects of heavy metal—Ozzy Osbourne, Tony Iommi, Geezer Butler, and Bill Ward—reunite on the Birmingham stage where it all began. This is more than a concert; it is the closing chapter of a 50-year saga that defined a genre and inspired generations. Witness the final, earth-shattering performance of the anthems that started it all.

Metallica

Paying the Toll. From covering "Sabbra Cadabra" to building their empire on Iommi's blueprint, Metallica's debt to Sabbath is immeasurable. The biggest metal band in the world returns to its roots, delivering a blistering set of pure, unadulterated thrash, honoring the masters who showed them the path to heavy.

Slayer

South of Heaven, East of Birmingham. While others played rock, Slayer and Sabbath explored the abyss. As titans of thrash, Slayer brings their unrelenting sonic assault to honor the band that first proved music could be truly dark and menacing. A tribute forged in the fires of aggression and absolute heaviness.

Pantera

A New Level of Respect. Pantera revolutionized metal with their power-groove, a direct descendant of Geezer Butler's seismic bass lines and Tony Iommi's street-level riffs. Now, the cowboys from hell unleash their hostile aggression to pay homage to the band that wrote the book on raw, untamed power.

Alice in Chains

Down in a Hole, Up on the Stage. No band carried the torch of Sabbath's dark, sludgy, and melodic despair into the 90s quite like Alice in Chains. Their sound, a haunting blend of heavy metal gloom and grunge introspection, is a perfect modern echo of Sabbath's earliest, most emotionally resonant work.

Gojira

The Art of Legacy. Proving that the spirit of innovation in heavy music is far from dead, Gojira brings their technically astounding and rhythmically seismic sound. Their performance is a testament to how Sabbath's groundbreaking spirit continues to empower modern bands to push the boundaries of what it means to be heavy.
